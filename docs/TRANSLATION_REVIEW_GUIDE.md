# Translation Review Guide

This guide is for human reviewers checking AXIS-NIDDHI translations.

The goal is not to make every sentence sound modern or casual. The goal is to
preserve meaning clearly enough that a reader is not misled.

## Important Note About the Portuguese Translation

This Portuguese version is offered with the best possible intention: to make the
teachings presented at PureDhamma.net more accessible to readers who are not
fluent in English.

However, these translations should be regarded as **study and review material**,
not as an official, final, or doctrinally certified translation.

Buddha Dhamma is profound and highly sensitive to precise terminology. Many
Pali terms - such as anicca, dukkha, anatta, sankhara, vinnana, tanha, avijja,
and others - do not have perfect one-word equivalents in Portuguese. An
imprecise translation may unintentionally distort the intended meaning.

Therefore:

- translation, nuance, context, or terminology errors may remain;
- the English articles at PureDhamma.net remain the primary reference for this
  version;
- whenever in doubt, readers should consult the original English article;
- any passage that appears unclear, contradictory, or unusual should be treated
  as a review point, not as a final conclusion.

This work is an ongoing Dhamma Dana offering. It should be reviewed tirelessly
as conditions allow. Until it is reviewed and validated by someone truly
qualified in Dhamma - ideally someone with direct realization of the Path - this
Portuguese version **should not be considered an official or final translation
of the teachings at PureDhamma.net**.

May any merit generated by this effort be shared with all beings. May any
errors be found and corrected quickly, without causing confusion on the Path.

ensinamentos apresentados em PureDhamma.net mais acessíveis a pessoas que não
leem inglês com fluência.

revisão**, não uma tradução oficial, final ou doutrinariamente certificada.

português. Uma tradução imprecisa pode facilmente alterar o sentido pretendido.

Por isso:

- podem existir erros de tradução, nuance, contexto ou escolha terminológica;
- o texto em inglês de PureDhamma.net permanece como a referência principal
  desta versão;
- em caso de dúvida, consulte sempre o artigo original em inglês;
- qualquer passagem que pareça estranha, contraditória ou confusa deve ser
  tratada como ponto de revisão, não como conclusão definitiva.

Este trabalho é uma oferta de Dhamma Dāna em andamento. Ele deverá ser revisado
incansavelmente enquanto houver condições para isso. Até que uma pessoa
verdadeiramente qualificada no Dhamma - idealmente alguém com realização direta
do Caminho - possa validar o conteúdo, esta versão em português **não deve ser
considerada uma tradução oficial ou final dos ensinamentos de PureDhamma.net**.

Que qualquer mérito gerado por este esforço seja compartilhado com todos os
seres. Que quaisquer erros sejam rapidamente encontrados, corrigidos e não
causem confusão no Caminho.

## Review Priorities

Check in this order:

1. Meaning: does the Portuguese preserve the doctrinal point?
2. Pali terms: are key terms preserved or rendered according to project policy?
3. Tone: does the text still feel like teaching material, not marketing copy?
4. Titles: does the title help the reader without adding interpretation?
5. Readability: can a careful reader follow the sentence?

## Terms That Need Special Care

These are common danger zones:

| Term | Review note |
|---|---|
| Anicca | Do not flatten to ordinary "impermanence" if that loses the PureDhamma sense. |
| Dukkha | Avoid reducing to ordinary pain or sadness when the context is deeper. |
| Anatta / Anatta | Avoid automatic "no-self" phrasing when it distorts the intended meaning. |
| Nibbana | Usually preserve the Pali form. |
| Paticca Samuppada | Preserve the technical term unless a reviewed explanation is present. |
| sankhara, vinnana, tanha, upadana | Check context before translating loosely. |

When unsure, prefer preserving the Pali term and adding review notes rather than
inventing a smooth but inaccurate equivalent.

## What to Record

For each issue, record:

- page title or PDPN code if visible;
- the English phrase;
- the Portuguese phrase;
- what feels wrong or risky;
- a suggested correction, if you have one;
- whether the issue is meaning, tone, grammar, title, or navigation.

## Good Review Comments

Helpful:

```text
KD.FF.014: "Anatta No Soul No Atma"
Issue: title translation risks implying a simple metaphysical slogan.
Suggestion: preserve "Anatta" and review whether "sem alma, sem atma" is enough
for this section.
```

Less helpful:

```text
This sounds bad.
```

## Review Outcome Labels

Use these plain labels in notes or issues:

- `OK`: acceptable as-is.
- `POLISH`: understandable, but wording can improve.
- `MEANING-RISK`: may distort the teaching.
- `PALI-RISK`: key term handling needs review.
- `TITLE-RISK`: title may mislead or over-interpret.
- `BLOCKER`: should not be published as reviewed.

## Guiding Principle

If a translation is elegant but changes the meaning, it fails. If it is slightly
plain but preserves the meaning, it can be improved later.
