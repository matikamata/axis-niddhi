# Welcome Letter to the Guardians (Kalyāṇamittas)

> *"The colors of the rainbow, so pretty in the sky..."*  
> — Listen: Louis Armstrong — What a Wonderful World

Welcome to **AXIS-NIDDHI** (also known as the *Brasileirinho Engine*, or `/beng`).

If you are here, something in this project resonated with you. 
Before diving into the code, there is something important to understand — not as a rule, but as an invitation.

---

## The Scale of the Challenge

The corpus from PureDhamma.net is vast: hundreds of dense, highly structured essays that aim to explain the mechanics of the mind and suffering with precision.

Translating and preserving this knowledge across languages — even with AI tools and structured glossaries — is a non-trivial task.

You **will** encounter imperfections:
- slightly unnatural phrasing
- minor grammatical inconsistencies
- literal translations where nuance is still evolving

This is expected.

---

## A Different Way to Engage

When encountering an error, there are always two possible reactions:

- Criticism, frustration, dismissal  
- Or contribution, refinement, improvement  

This project is built on the second path.

Instead of asking:
> “Why is this wrong?”

You may ask:
> “How can I improve this for everyone who comes after me?”

That small shift transforms passive consumption into active contribution.

---

## The Flower Does Not Chase the Bees

AXIS-NIDDHI follows a simple principle:

> The flower opens. It does not chase.

This repository exists as an offering:
- no pressure to contribute
- no expectation of participation
- no ownership over outcomes

If it resonates, you are welcome.

If not, that is perfectly fine.

---

## If You Choose to Participate

You are invited to act as a **Kalyāṇa mitta** — a “noble friend” who helps ensure that translations are not only correct, but meaningful.

No force. No urgency.

Just careful, voluntary contribution.
