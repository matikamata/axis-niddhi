# Proposal for Authorization Request — AXIS-NIDDHI

This document is a formal draft intended for Prof. Lal A. Pinnaduwala. It focuses on the mission of preserving the PureDhamma.net corpus for future generations using the AXIS-NIDDHI engine.

---

**Subject:** Request for Blessing & Authorization: Static Preservation Archive (AXIS-NIDDHI)

**Dear Prof. Lal A. Pinnaduwala,**

I am writing to you as a dedicated student of the Dhamma and a deep admirer of the clarity you have brought to the Buddha Dhamma through PureDhamma.net. Your work has been a lighthouse for many seeking the truth across the globe.

I am the creator of **AXIS-NIDDHI**, a non-commercial technical project designed to serve as a "timeless, static mirror" of the PureDhamma.net corpus.

### The Purpose of AXIS-NIDDHI
The world is increasingly digital, yet digital platforms are inherently fragile. Websites depend on servers, domain renewals, and constant connectivity. 

The AXIS-NIDDHI engine was built to ensure that even in the absence of internet connectivity or server-side infrastructure, your teachings remain accessible. It is an **offline-first, zero-dependency preservation** that can be read from a USB drive, a local network, or a static IPFS node — ensuring the Dhamma can endure through shifting technical landscapes.

### Our Commitment to Integrity
We adhere to the FOLLOWING strict principles:
1.  **Immutability:** No content has been altered. No teaching has been reinterpreted. We preserve your words exactly as published.
2.  **No Competition:** This project is not a replacement for PureDhamma.net. It is explicitly labeled as a "Preservation Mirror" and includes links back to your original site as the primary source of truth.
3.  **No Commercialization:** This is a 100% voluntary, open-source project. There are no ads, no trackers, and no monetization.
4.  **Bilingual Reach:** We have integrated a translation engine (DeepL) to help students in Brazil (and eventually other regions) access your work, always emphasizing the need for human review by students of the Dhamma.

### A Request for Your Blessing
We humbly ask for your formal authorization to maintain this preservation mirror. We see ourselves as "Kalyāṇamittas" (noble friends) and guardians of the archives, not as owners. 

Our only goal is to ensure that the *Hetu-Phala* and *Paṭicca Samuppāda* teachings you have so meticulously documented are available for as long as man-made files can be read.

We are ready to adjust any aspect of the presentation according to your wishes. If you feel this project serves the Dhamma, your blessing would mean the world to our small community of students.

With deep respect, gratitude, and Mettā,

**[Your Name]**  
*On behalf of the AXIS-NIDDHI Team*
